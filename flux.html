Using the Flux cluster

Flux is the main, shared computing cluster for the University of Michigan. It is operated by ARC-TS, and faculty, staff, and students can get access to it.

Flux has large-memory (up to 1.5 TB RAM) and GPU nodes. Flux is a for-fee service, and two fee models are available for the standard Flux, monthly subscription and per-CPU second. Flux is a good resource for medium- to large-scale tasks where computations per task take more than an hour. Some neuroimaging software is preinstalled on Flux, e.g., FSL, FreeSurfer, and others can be installed.

Past uses of Flux include running FreeSurfer's recon-all on approximately 130 subjects, where average per-subject processing time was 9.5 hours. Total processing time on a single machine was estimated to take more than 1200 hours, or about 50-some days. Using a Flux allocation of 12 processors, it was completed in about six days, less a few problematic subjects.
